Links and Resources

"No matter where you go, you are always connected."

Mathematics and Homework Help

  • Paul's Notes - Very well written and annotated lecture notes beginning with algebra/ precalculus up to multivariable calculus and differential equations.
  • Visual Math Learning - This is more appropriate for middle and high school students. A lot of students are turned off by large sequences of symbols and are liable to miss the conceptual framework of what they're learning. Visual math is a good way to re-engage students in the material and make sense of the symbols!
  • Group Props Wiki - A fantastic wiki compiled by a math grad student documenting various known group properties. I used this extensively when studying modern algebra in college!
  • Khan Academy - Okay there's more here than just mathematics. Videos of discussions and tutorials on a cornucopia of topics that walk you though everything. Especially math!
  • CTY AP and College Resources - Useful hub and FAQ's for AP calculus students and beginning college students!
  • Penn State Calculus Apps - A large collection of java applets visually demonstrating calculus concepts. Make sure you have a java plugin for your browser.
  • ODE Videos - Series of short instructional videos on Ordinary Differential Equations.
  • Math TV - Fantastic lectures on a restricted selection of math topics on youtube.

Information Technology

  • Google A+ videos - A+ certification is your first step into the world of IT. The study guide is huge and can seem intimidating. But there are a plethora of online guides and tutorials to midigate the effect.
  • Professor Messer's A+ Course - You have no idea how thankfull I am for this.
  • Messer's Network + course - and this, too!

Coding and Web Development/Design

  • A List Apart - Periodical on web design.
  • HTML5 Doctor - An annotated index of all HTML5 elements with examples.
  • Site Point - References and resources for web development technologies: html, css, js, php, and more!
  • Codecademy - Free online learning tracks for some of the most relevent languages and markup languages today.
  • Codeschool - Not free, but more comprehensive than Codecademy and with more content.
  • Mathjax - Embedding LaTeX into html files. What I'm using to write math tutorials on here!
