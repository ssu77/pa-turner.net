About me and pa-turner.net

I've never been amazing at writing about myself. Never cared too much for it either. I've always been more interested in learning about other people, their stories, and their achievement.

At any rate, I went to high school at Inglemoor High School, class of 2006, where I participated in the IB program and began developing my initial interests in mathematics and technology. Thereafter I enrolled at a junior college and transferred to the University of Washington, Seattle as a mathematics major. After completing course work in modern algebra, analysis and topology, as well as in computer programming, applied math, and statistics I finally graduated in 2012 with my Bachelor of Science.

My professional and volunteer activities during and after college have been many and diverse. I received formal training in tutoring and the "mentor-student relationship" from America Reads America Counts when I first began working for them as part of my work study in college. I TA'd in several math classrooms and conducted after school math help sessions at Kenmore Junior High. Since then I've been doing free lance tutoring as well. It started out several years ago as a favor for friends and family and began to grow.

I'm a volunteer for the Providence Hospice of Seattle. I spend up to four hours each week visiting with patients and their families/ caregivers to provide companionship and respite. In addition to this I also volunteer for the Virupa Educational Institute at the Sakya Monastery of Tibetan Buddhism where I help set up for classes, record lectures, and manage the sound board.

Outside of math, education, and technology, my other long term interests include Linux, Tibetan Buddhism, analytic philosophy, and inventing things in Minecraft. My favorite books are Godel, Escher, Bach - An Eternal Golden Braid, Science and Sanity, The Problems of Philosophy, and Cryptonomicon.
